# B002 | Failure Handling — Missed Deadline or Project Failure

**Category:** Failure & Ownership  
**Difficulty:** Hard  
**Time Limit:** 5 minutes (spoken) | 10 minutes (written)  
**Commonly asked at:** Amazon (LP: Ownership), Google, Meta

---

## The Question

> **"Tell me about a time when you failed to meet a deadline or a project did not go as planned. What happened, and what did you do about it?"**

---

## Follow-up Questions

1. What was the root cause — and did you see it coming?
2. What did you communicate to stakeholders, and when?
3. What systems or processes did you put in place afterward to prevent recurrence?
4. How did this affect your team's trust in you, and how did you rebuild it?

---

## Scoring Rubric

| Dimension | 1 — Poor | 2 — Fair | 3 — Good | 4 — Exceptional |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Ownership** | Deflects blame to others/requirements | Partially owns it | Clear ownership, no excuses | Full ownership + proactive communication |
| **Crisis Response** | Panicked or hid the problem | Reported late | Reported promptly with a plan | Early signal + mitigation plan ready |
| **Growth** | No learning mentioned | Vague "I'll be more careful" | Specific change in behavior | Systemic change implemented + measurable result |

---

## Red Flags That Will Tank Your Score

- Blaming unclear requirements, teammates, or management.
- Choosing a "failure" that isn't a real failure (humble-brag).
- Waiting until the last minute to escalate.
- No concrete change in behavior mentioned afterward.
- "We" instead of "I" throughout the entire answer.

---

## Tips for a 4-Point Answer

- **Pick a real failure** — interviewers can smell manufactured modesty.
- **Show early escalation**: The best engineers communicate problems as soon as they're detected, not at the deadline.
- **Focus on the system, not willpower**: "I decided to work harder" is a 1-point answer. "I introduced weekly scope-review checkpoints going forward" is a 4-point answer.
- **Quantify the recovery**: How many days late? What was the business cost? What improved afterward?
